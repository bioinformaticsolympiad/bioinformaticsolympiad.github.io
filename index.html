🧬 About the Olympiad

What is BBO 3.0?

A national-level competition promoting scientific excellence and modern bioinformatics.

The Flagship Event

The Biology & Bioinformatics Olympiad 3.0 is a national-level competition organized by BioPC. Following the tremendous success of the previous edition, which attracted nearly 3,000 participants from universities across Bangladesh, BioPC is proud to launch the third edition.

The Olympiad aims to inspire students in the life sciences, promote scientific excellence, and provide a platform for exploring modern biology and bioinformatics through competitive learning and problem-solving.

🎯 Eligibility

Who Can Participate?

Open to undergraduate and graduate students from any recognized university studying Biology, Biotechnology, Genetics, Microbiology, Bioinformatics, Pharmacy, Zoology, Botany, MBBS, Environmental Science, or any other Life Science discipline. Students from all universities and all academic years are welcome!

❓ Common FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the 3rd Biology & Bioinformatics Olympiad.

1 Is there any registration fee?

Online round is completely free. If you qualify for the second round, you will have to pay a little fees for offline program support.

2 What is the registration deadline?

The registration deadline is June 30, 2026 at 11:59 PM.

3 Which platforms will be used for the online round?

The online round will be conducted through Google Meet, TestMoz, and LiveMCQ.

4 Where will the final round take place?

The final round will be held at the University of Chittagong (CU).

5 What topics will be covered in the Olympiad?

Questions may come from Biology, Molecular Biology, Genetics, Biotechnology, Microbiology, Bioinformatics, Biochemistry, Zoology, Botany, Environmental Science, and related Life Science fields.

6 Will there be Bioinformatics questions for beginners?

Yes. The Olympiad will include both fundamental and advanced-level questions so students from different academic backgrounds can participate comfortably.

7 Is teamwork allowed?

No. The Olympiad is an individual competition.

8 How will participants be selected for the final round?

Participants with the highest scores in the online elimination round will qualify for the offline final round.

9 What should I prepare before the online round?

Participants should ensure a stable internet connection, a charged device, and access to the required platforms before the exam starts.

10 Can freshers or first-year students participate?

Absolutely. Students from all academic years are encouraged to join.
